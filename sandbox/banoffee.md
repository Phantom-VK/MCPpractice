# Banoffee Pie

*A classic British dessert combining banana, toffee (caramel), and cream on a buttery pastry base.*

**Source:** BBC Good Food — by Miriam Nice  
**Rating:** ★ 4.8 / 5 (16 ratings)  
**Serves:** 8–10  
**Difficulty:** Easy  
**Prep:** 30 mins | **Cook:** 30–35 mins

---

## Ingredients

### For the Pastry
- **100g** butter, chilled (plus extra for greasing)
- **200g** plain flour
- **1** medium egg, separated
- **1 tbsp** golden caster sugar

### For the Filling & Topping
- **394g** caramel or dulce de leche
- **4** bananas, sliced
- **300ml** double cream
- Dark chocolate, finely grated (optional, for garnish)

---

## Method

1. **Make the pastry dough.** Put the butter and flour in a food processor and pulse until it resembles fresh breadcrumbs. Add the egg yolk and sugar and pulse again until mixed through. Add very cold water, 1 tbsp at a time, pulsing after each addition. Stop adding water as soon as the dough starts to come together as one piece.

2. **Chill the dough.** Remove the dough from the processor and gently knead by hand into a smooth ball. Wrap tightly in cling film and chill in the fridge for **30 minutes**.

3. **Line the tin.** Heat oven to **190°C / 170°C fan / Gas 4**. Grease a 23cm loose-bottomed fluted tart tin. Roll out the chilled pastry on a lightly floured surface and use it to line the tin, leaving about ½ cm of pastry proud of the edge — snip to length with kitchen scissors.

4. **Bake the pastry case.** Line the base with baking parchment and weigh down with baking beans or uncooked rice. Bake for **15 minutes**, then remove the paper and beans and brush the pastry surface with beaten egg white. Return to the oven for a further **15–20 minutes** until crisp, golden, and cooked through. Leave to cool completely (can be done a day ahead).

5. **Assemble.** Spread half of the caramel over the base of the cooled pastry case. Layer the sliced bananas on top, then cover with the remaining caramel. Place in the fridge.

6. **Finish and serve.** Whip the cream until billowy thick and spoon it over the top of the tart. Finely grate over 1–2 pieces of dark chocolate to garnish.

---

## Tips

- The pastry case can be baked a day ahead to save time.
- For a quicker version, swap the pastry for a crushed biscuit base (digestive biscuits mixed with melted butter).
- Best served freshly made, but can be kept in the fridge overnight — ideally add the whipped cream on the day of serving.
